# Dating & authenticating vintage womenswear

**Rule: use at least 3 independent cues** (label + zipper + care tag, etc.) before committing
to a decade. State which cues you used so the dating is defensible.

## Cue-by-cue

**Union labels**
- **ILGWU** (International Ladies' Garment Workers' Union): no "AFL-CIO" = pre-1959; "AFL-CIO"
  present = 1959–1974; an **"R" (registered) symbol** appears after 1964; red/white/blue label =
  1974–1995; "UNITE!" = 1995+ (most don't consider UNITE!-era "vintage" yet).
- **ACWA / ACTWU**: tailored items; ACWA ran 1934–1976, then merged into ACTWU in 1976.
- **"Made in Canada" set vertically** on the label = Canadian union piece.
- **No union label** can mean pre-union, imported, or simply a non-union maker — don't over-read it.

**RN / WPL numbers**
- RN system starts 1952. 5 digits = roughly 1950s–70s. 6+ digits = 1980s onward.
- (WPL numbers are the older wool-products equivalent; their presence skews earlier.)

**Care labels**
- Absent pre-1971. Mandatory (written instructions) from 1972. Care *symbols* from 1997.

**Fabric content tags**
- Required in the US from 1960; fiber percentages appear post-1960.

**Zipper type & placement**
- Metal (Talon, Conmar, Crown), side seam or center back = 1930s–60s.
- **Nylon coil**, center-back = appears in the **1960s** (mod styles).
- Plastic / nylon (YKK, Scovill, Talon Zephyr), center back = 1970s onward.

**Seam & stitch construction**
- French seams, hand-finished, pinked edges = **pre-1950s**.
- Pinked seams = 1950s. Serged / overlocked = mid-1960s onward.
- Single-stitch / hand-rolled hems read older and higher-quality; double-stitch/serged = 1960s+.

**Country of origin**
- "Made in USA" required post-1971. Imports rise sharply through the 1980s.
- **"Made in British Hong Kong"** = 1945–1997. **"Made in Japan"** (on womenswear) = 1950s–1970s.

**Copyright dates**
- On novelty/union tags and **licensed prints** (cartoon characters, band art, etc.), the printed
  date pinpoints the year (or shortly after) of manufacture. Always photograph it.

## Decade quick-reference

| Decade | Union label | RN/WPL | Care label | Zipper | Stitching | Country label |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1950s | ILGWU shield, no "AFL-CIO" | 00101–04086 | None | Metal, side/back | Single | None |
| 1960s | ILGWU "AFL-CIO" | 13,670+ | None/rare | Metal/plastic, center back | Double | None / pre-1971 |
| 1970s | ILGWU red/white/blue | 13,670–55,000 | Required | Plastic/nylon, center back | Double | "Made in USA" |
| 1980s | ILGWU / ACTWU | 6 digits, start "1" | Required | Plastic/nylon | Double | "Made in USA" / imports |
| 1990s | UNITE! | 6+ digits, start "1" | Required | Plastic/nylon | Double | "Made in USA" rare / imports |

**Worked example:** ILGWU "AFL-CIO" shield + 5-digit RN + no care label → early 1960s.

## Authentication notes
- Spot designer/high-value labels early (see brand tiers in `pricing-fees-sourcing.md`) — they
  change pricing strategy and may qualify for eBay's Authenticity Guarantee on apparel.
- If cues conflict (e.g., a replaced zipper), weight the labels and stitching over the zipper
  and describe it as "circa," explaining the conflict rather than overclaiming.
