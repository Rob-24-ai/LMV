# Pricing, fees & sourcing

## How to price
1. Run eBay **Sold/Completed** comps. Never use active asking prices.
2. Filter by era + brand + style + condition.
3. Take the **median of ~8–15 comps** from the last **60–120 days** (recent + enough volume to
   be real), then adjust for condition and rarity.

### Rarity multipliers (apply after condition)
| Rarity | Multiplier |
|---|---|
| Uncommon | 1.3–1.5× |
| Rare | 3–5× |
| Extremely rare | 5–20× |

## Condition adjustment (vs. a "Very Good" baseline)
| Condition | Adjustment |
|---|---|
| Mint | +40 to +100% |
| Excellent | +15 to +30% |
| Very Good | baseline |
| Good | −20 to −35% |
| Fair | −40 to −60% |

## Brand value tiers (2026)
| Tier | Example labels | Typical range |
|---|---|---|
| Top / designer | Chanel, Dior, YSL, Gucci, Hermès, Versace, Burberry, Pucci, Halston, Bob Mackie, Norma Kamali, Cacharel, DVF, Lilly Pulitzer | $100–$5,000+ |
| High | Gunne Sax, Betsey Johnson, Lilli Ann | $80–$1,500+ |
| Mid / "sleeper" | Levi's & Wrangler (esp. pre-1980s), vintage Nike/Adidas, Carhartt, Dickies, Champion Reverse Weave, Patagonia, North Face (90s/00s), Bill Blass, H Bar C | $40–$500+ |
| Entry / unbranded-desirable | 70s boho dresses, 80s blazers, 90s flannels, Y2K mesh tops, 60s mod minis, 50s circle skirts | $20–$150+ |

(Tier placement isn't rigid — a mint, rare Gunne Sax or Betsey Johnson piece can clear top-tier
prices. Let the sold comps, not the tier label, set the number.)

## Dollar examples
- 1970s Gunne Sax prairie dress, excellent: **$200–$400**
- 1980s Betsey Johnson party dress, good: **$80–$200**
- Unbranded 1940s rayon day dress, mint: **$120–$250**

## Auction vs. fixed price
- **BIN + Best Offer** for almost everything (one-of-a-kind items reward patience).
- **Auction** only for genuinely rare, high-demand pieces where competitive bidding helps.

## eBay fees (2025–2026)
| Fee | Value |
|---|---|
| Final value fee (apparel) | 13.25% (no store **or** Starter store) / 12.35% (Basic store & up) |
| Per-order fee | $0.40 on orders over $10, $0.30 on orders ≤ $10 |
| Below-Standard penalty | +6% (rises to +7% after 4+ months) |
| Insertion fee | $0 for first 250/mo, then $0.30/listing |
| Promoted Listings | 2–10% (charged only if it sells via the ad) |

A general fee increase of up to ~0.35% landed in Feb 2025 — the figures above already reflect it.

### Store subscription tiers
| Store | Monthly | Free listings/mo | Apparel FVF |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | $4.95 | 250 | 13.25% (no FVF discount) |
| Basic | $27.95 | 1,000 | 12.35% |
| Premium | $74.95 | 10,000 | 12.35% |
| Anchor | $349.95 | 25,000+ | 12.35% |

**Note:** the 12.35% discounted apparel FVF kicks in at **Basic and above** — a Starter store still
pays 13.25%. The math to clear Basic ($27.95/mo) is worth it once monthly sales are high enough
that the 0.9% FVF saving + extra free listings beat the subscription.

**Break-even reminder:** before pricing, subtract final value fee + per-order fee + any promoted %
+ shipping (if free-shipped) + cost of goods. Quote Rob the real margin, not the sale price.

## Sourcing locations & ROI
| Method | Cost/item | ROI | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Goodwill Outlet / "the bins" | $1–$2 | Very high | Best for bulk; pay by the pound |
| Wholesale bales / lots | $6–$10 | High | Consistent, scalable supply |
| Estate sales | $5–$20 | High | Best for rare/unique finds |
| Flea markets | $5–$15 | Med–high | Room to negotiate / bundle |
| Online auctions | $5–$20 | Med–high | Bulk, variable quality |

**Beginner-friendly eras/categories:** Y2K, 90s grunge/minimalist, 80s bold prints, vintage
tees/denim, unbranded boho/mod.

## Tax: 1099-K
eBay issues a 1099-K once sales cross the IRS reporting threshold. That threshold has been
**moving every year** (phased down toward $600), so don't hard-code it — **verify the current
year's threshold** before telling Rob whether he'll get a form. Either way, track cost-of-goods
and shipping from day one so reported gross isn't mistaken for profit.
