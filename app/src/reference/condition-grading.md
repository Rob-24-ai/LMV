# Condition grading & flaw description

Honest, specific flaw disclosure is the #1 defense against SNAD ("Significantly Not As
Described") claims. List every flaw in the description **and** show it in a photo.

## Grade definitions
| Grade | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Excellent | Minimal wear, no significant flaws |
| Very Good | Minor flaws (faint mark, tiny snag), presents well (pricing baseline) |
| Good | Obvious flaws (small stains, visible repairs), still wearable |
| Fair | Heavy wear, multiple flaws — best for costume/restoration buyers |

## Flaw vocabulary (neutral, factual, trusted by buyers)
Name the flaw, size it, and locate it precisely:
- "Light foxing" — small, pale age spots
- "Pea-sized yellow mark, left cuff"
- "Two pinholes, back hem" (for tiny moth holes — "small moth nibble" also works)
- "Gentle fading at shoulders"
- "Light pilling under arms"
- "Faint yellowing / discoloration at underarms" (UA shadowing)
- "Hand-stitched repair at side seam, not visible when worn"
- "Loose seam at hem"
- Odor: "No detectable odor" or "Faint musty scent, airing recommended" — never hide it.

## Best practices
- List **all** flaws up front, in both description and photos.
- Point to the photo: "See photo 4 for small stain on sleeve."
- Reassure honestly: "Presents beautifully overall. Flaws do not detract from wearability."
- **Never** edit a flaw out of a photo. Adjust brightness/white-balance only.
