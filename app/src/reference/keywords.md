# Era & style keywords that sell (2025–2026)

Buyers search by **era + aesthetic**, not the word "vintage." Pick the cluster that matches
the garment and layer 4–7 of its terms into the title and item specifics.

## Keyword clusters
- **Y2K** — y2k, y2k grunge, cyber y2k, mcbling, juicy couture, mesh, rhinestone
- **90s grunge** — 90s, grunge, baby tee, acid wash, slip dress, plaid
- **70s boho** — 70s, boho, lagenlook, prairie, embroidered, festival
- **60s mod** — 60s, mod, shift dress, Audrey Hepburn, geometric
- **50s rockabilly** — 50s, rockabilly, circle skirt, swing, pin-up
- **80s power** — 80s, power suit, padded shoulder, bold print, statement
- **Cottagecore** — cottagecore, prairie, grandmacore, lace, floral
- **Dark academia** — dark academia, plaid, wool, preppy, academia

**Why era keywords beat "vintage":** they carry the search volume. E.g. "Y2K corset top" runs
~6,700 searches/mo vs. ~2,900 for "vintage corset top" — and the era term often pulls a higher
sale price too. Lead with the era/aesthetic, not the word "vintage."

## Layering in titles
Structure: `[Brand] [Garment Type] [Size] [Color] [Era/Style] [Silhouette/Detail]`
Example: `Gunne Sax 70s Prairie Maxi Dress Lace Floral White Blue Boho Cottagecore 7`

**How many keywords (the reconciled rule):** use **1–2 *era/aesthetic* keywords** — never stack
decades (no "Y2K 90s 80s 70s"). That's the cap that matters. Then fill the rest of the 80 chars
with *factual descriptors* — material, color, pattern, silhouette, closure — which are not
"keyword stuffing" because they're physically true of the garment. So the Gunne Sax example uses
two aesthetic terms (boho, cottagecore) plus descriptors (prairie, maxi, lace, floral, white/blue).

## Trending vs. declining
- **Trending (2025–2026):** Y2K, 90s grunge, boho, cottagecore, dark academia, minimalist 90s.
- **Declining:** graphic tees, vague trend words ("viral," bare "core").

## eBay title rules
- Max ~6–7 style keywords — no stuffing.
- No ALL CAPS, no store name, no repeated words.
- Only physically verifiable descriptors (don't tag "boho" on a mod shift to chase traffic).
